What are Rom-Coms?

Rom-Com stands for Romantic comedy, often abbreviated as "romcom," is a popular genre in anime that combines elements of romance and humor. These series typically revolve around the romantic relationships between characters, often in a lighthearted and humorous manner. Romantic comedy anime, or romcom, is a popular genre blending romance and humor. Common features include high school settings, misunderstandings, love triangles, quirky characters, tsundere archetypes, clich√©s, slice of life elements, physical comedy, and unconventional premises. Despite the comedic focus, romcoms also incorporate heartwarming moments to explore genuine emotions and character growth.

With that foundation laid, let's dive into the realm of anime and explore the top 10 best romantic comedy series ever.

Top 10: "Nisekoi: False Love"

  • Author: Naoshi Komi
  • Studio: Shaft
  • Year of release: 2014
  • Genre: Romance, Comedy, Shonen

The story revolves around Raku Ichijou, the heir to the yakuza family, and Chitoge Kirisaki, the daughter of a rival gangster group. To prevent a gang war, Raku and Chitoge are forced into a fake relationship, acting as a couple to maintain peace between their families. The catch is that Raku has a locket that can only be opened by the key Chitoge wears. This leads to a series of comedic situations, misunderstandings, and romantic developments as they navigate the challenges of their pretend relationship.

Top 9: "Golden Time"

  • Author: Yuyuko Takemiya
  • Studio: J.C. Staff
  • Year of release: 2013
  • Genre: Romance, Comedy, Drama, Slice Of Life

The story follows Tada Banri, a freshman law student at a private law school in Tokyo. Banri has lost his memories due to an accident, and he is trying to start a new life in the bustling city. Early in the story, he encounters Mitsuo Yanagisawa, a fellow freshman, who is trying to escape from a persistent childhood friend, Koko Kaga. As Banri and Mitsuo navigate their new lives, they become entangled in a complex web of relationships, with Banri finding himself caught between his past and present.

Top 8: "The Pet Girl of Sakurasou (Sakurasou no Pet na Kanojo)"

  • Author: Hajime Kamoshida
  • Studio: J.C. Staff
  • Year of release: 2012
  • Genre: Romance, Comedy, Drama

The story revolves around Sorata Kanda, a high school student who is forced to move into the Sakura Hall dormitory after being unable to handle the responsibility of taking care of stray cats in his regular dorm. Sakura Hall is known for housing eccentric and talented students, including Mashiro Shiina, a world-renowned artist with exceptional artistic abilities but lacking in basic life skills. Sorata becomes Mashiro's caretaker, earning him the nickname "master," and the series follows their experiences alongside other quirky residents of Sakura Hall.

Top 7: "Clannad: After Story"

  • Authors: Jun Maeda, Kai, and OdiakeS
  • Studio: Kyoto Animation
  • Year of release: 2008
  • Genre: Romance, Comedy, Drama, Slice Of Life

The story picks up where "Clannad" left off and follows Tomoya Okazaki and Nagisa Furukawa as they transition into adulthood. The narrative explores the challenges of married life, parenthood, and the impact of past decisions on the characters' present and future. The series introduces supernatural elements, including the mysterious Illusionary World, which plays a crucial role in the overarching narrative.

Top 6: "Ore Monogatari!! (My Love Story!!)"

  • Author: Kazune Kawahara
  • Studio: Madhouse
  • Year of release: 2015
  • Genre: Romance, Comedy, Slice Of Life

The story follows Takeo Gouda, a high school student known for his immense physical strength and intimidating appearance. Despite his kind and selfless nature, Takeo has always been overlooked by girls who are more drawn to his handsome best friend, Makoto Sunakawa. However, everything changes when Takeo saves Rinko Yamato, a sweet and gentle girl, from a harasser on a train. Unbeknownst to Takeo, Rinko falls in love with him, leading to a unique and heartwarming romantic journey.

Top 5: "Monthly Girls' Nozaki-kun (Gekkan Shoujo Nozaki-kun)"

  • Author: Izumi Tsubaki
  • Studio: Doga Kobo
  • Year of release: 2014
  • Genre: Romance, Comedy, Slice Of Life, Shoujo

"Monthly Girls' Nozaki-kun" follows the story of Chiyo Sakura, a high school student who has a crush on her classmate, Umetarou Nozaki. When she confesses her feelings to him, Nozaki, instead of accepting or rejecting her, hands her an autograph. Chiyo discovers that Nozaki is a famous shoujo manga artist known by the pen name Sakiko Yumeno. She ends up becoming his assistant, and the series humorously explores the behind-the-scenes process of creating manga, as well as the various romantic and comedic situations that arise among the characters.

Top 4: "Kaguya-sama: Love is War (Kaguya-sama wa Kokurasetai)"

  • Author: Aka Akasaka
  • Studio: A-1 Pictures
  • Year of release: 2019
  • Genre: Romance, Comedy, School, Psychological

"Kaguya-sama: Love is War" revolves around the student council of Shuchiin Academy, known for its high-achieving and talented students. The protagonists are Kaguya Shinomiya, the vice president, and Miyuki Shirogane, the president. Both are highly intelligent and have developed feelings for each other, but their pride prevents them from confessing their love. The series hilariously depicts their attempts to make the other confess first, leading to a "war" of wits and psychological battles.

Top 3: "Love, Chunibyo & Other Delusions (Chuunibyou demo Koi ga Shitai!)"

  • Author: Torako
  • Studio: Kyoto Animation
  • Year of release: 2012
  • Genre: Romance, Comedy, Drama, Slice Of Life

"Love, Chunibyo & Other Delusions" follows the story of Yuuta Togashi, a high school student who once suffered from "chunibyo" syndrome, a condition where individuals exhibit delusional behavior and often believe they possess supernatural powers. Embarrassed by his past, Yuuta decides to start fresh in high school and avoid any associations with his chunibyo days. However, he encounters Rikka Takanashi, a fellow student who is still deeply immersed in her own chunibyo fantasies. The series explores the comedic and heartwarming moments as Yuuta gets entangled in Rikka's imaginative world, leading to a mix of romance and self-discovery.

Top 2: "My Teen Romantic Comedy SNAFU (Oregairu)"

  • Author: Wataru Watari
  • Studio: Brain's Base and Feel
  • Year of release: 2013
  • Genre: Romance, Comedy, Drama, Slice Of Life

The story follows Hachiman Hikigaya, a high school student with a cynical outlook on life and a preference for solitude. As part of a school-mandated service club, Hachiman is joined by the bright and popular Yukino Yukinoshita and the cheerful Yui Yuigahama. The trio is tasked with helping their classmates with various issues, leading to insightful and often introspective commentary on social dynamics, relationships, and the challenges of adolescence.

Top 1: Toradora!

  • Author: Yuyuko Takemiya
  • Studio: J.C. Staff
  • Year of release: 2008
  • Genre: Romance, Comedy, Drama, Slice Of Life

The story revolves around Ryuji Takasu, a high school student with a delinquent appearance but a kind heart. Taiga Aisaka, known as the "Palmtop Tiger" due to her small stature and feisty personality, is Ryuji's classmate. Despite their contrasting personalities, they form an unlikely alliance to help each other pursue their respective crushes. As they spend more time together, their relationship becomes more complex, and the series explores themes of love, friendship, and self-discovery.
